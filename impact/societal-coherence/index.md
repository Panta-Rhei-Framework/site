## Scope

This section concerns fragmentation of knowledge, science-versus-faith tension, divided-house epistemologies, public discourse around meaning and reality, and coherence between scientific and existential registers.

## Core Idea

If the framework holds, it may provide not only scientific answers, but also a stronger architecture for reconciling tensions that modern societies often treat as permanent. That remains a conditional claim. It does not imply instant agreement, institutional adoption, or social repair by argument alone.

## Possible Coherence Consequences

- Better distinctions between formal proof, empirical adequacy, and interpretive commitment.
- A less adversarial relation between scientific and humanistic explanation.
- A public vocabulary for asking "what follows?" without collapsing every register into one.
- A way to discuss meaning without leaving scientific seriousness behind.

## Important Note

Societal coherence is the most fragile impact stratum. It must remain non-triumphalist, explicitly conditional, and open to the possibility that the framework is wrong, partial, or socially less useful than intellectually interesting.
