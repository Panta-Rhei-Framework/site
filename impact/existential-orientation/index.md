## Scope

This section concerns self-understanding, the relation between mind and reality, meaning and cosmos, human place within a coherent world-picture, and existential interpretation of a unified framework.

## Core Idea

If the framework holds, its consequences may extend beyond technical science into how humans orient themselves in relation to being, life, mind, and meaning. That does not make existential interpretation a proof surface. It remains a conditional consequence surface downstream of the more formal layers.

## Possible Consequences

- A more structured account of mind and self-description.
- A different relation between life, consciousness, and the cosmos.
- A clearer boundary between formal proof, empirical claim, and existential commitment.
- A less fragmented way of reading scientific and humanistic questions together.

## Boundary Condition

This page does not claim that existential meaning can be mechanically derived. It identifies possible orientation consequences if the framework survives the checks named elsewhere.
