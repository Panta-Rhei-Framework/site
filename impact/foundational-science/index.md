## Scope

This section concerns impact on mathematical foundations, foundational physics, philosophy of science, cross-domain explanatory standards, and the scientific worldview at the level of foundations.

## Core Idea

If the framework is valid, it could alter what counts as a viable unification architecture. The impact would not be "one more theory among many" but a shift in what must be shown: traceable definitions, explicit assumptions, registry-backed claims, verification posture, and problem-level consequences.

## Possible Consequences

- **Mathematical foundations**: a constructive, internally disciplined route from kernel to arithmetic, geometry, and spectral structure.
- **Theoretical physics**: a no-knobs calibration posture that makes falsification pressure unusually concentrated.
- **Philosophy of science**: a stronger distinction between coherence, empirical adequacy, and existential interpretation.
- **Methodology**: public source objects, formal verification, and release manifests become part of scientific legibility.

## Boundaries

Foundational impact remains conditional on survival under formal, empirical, and expert review. The lane does not claim disciplinary adoption; it names the consequences that would become live if the framework holds.
