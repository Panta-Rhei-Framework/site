## What this is

Panta Rhei is an independent open research program asking whether one constrained formal kernel can support a unified model across mathematics, physics, life, and metaphysics.

The site is not a blog, a marketing page, or a documentation dump. It is a structured public interface to a living research system.

## First reading path

Start here if you are new:

1. [Program]({{ '/program/' | relative_url }}) — what is being attempted, and what burden of proof follows.
2. [Corpus]({{ '/corpus/' | relative_url }}) — what has been built as structured research material.
3. [Results]({{ '/results/' | relative_url }}) — what the framework claims follows.
4. [Verify]({{ '/verify/' | relative_url }}) — how to inspect, reproduce, challenge, or falsify.
5. [Publications]({{ '/publications/' | relative_url }}) — released books, notes, papers, ledgers, and errata.

## For reviewers

If you are evaluating the program rather than casually browsing, start with [Verify]({{ '/verify/' | relative_url }}), then move to the [Corpus]({{ '/corpus/registry/' | relative_url }}) and the [Results]({{ '/results/problem-ledger/' | relative_url }}). The right first question is not whether to believe the framework; it is whether the work has earned serious structured scrutiny.

## For domain readers

Readers coming from a specific field may prefer the [World Readout]({{ '/results/world-readout/' | relative_url }}). It gathers the program's domain-facing claims into mathematics, physics, life, and metaphysics.
