> Defines the minimal formal core from which the rest of the Corpus is built.

<div class="notice note"><strong>Status note.</strong> Build status reflects the current internal state of the Corpus. It does not imply external acceptance unless explicitly stated.</div>

## What this step is required to do

The program must define a kernel precise enough to build from without importing mathematics, physics, life, or metaphysics as finished inputs.

## What the Corpus built

The Corpus presents the five generators, progression operator, K0-K6 axioms, boundary behavior, and diagonal/resource discipline of the tau-kernel.

## Why this step is required

The program cannot begin by assuming a finished mathematical or physical background. A candidate no-externalities framework must first say what its starting structure is, how that structure generates objects, and what counts as admissible construction.

## Key constructions

- The five primitive generators.
- The global progression operator.
- K0-K6 as the initial kernel axiom cluster.
- Boundary and fixed-point behavior.
- Diagonal and resource discipline.
